About Me

This is what I do.

As an avid reader and book lover, I have always been drawn to the magic and escapism of the fantasy genre. There's something truly special about getting lost in a world filled with mythical creatures, ancient magic, and epic adventures. For me, there's nothing quite like the feeling of being completely immersed in a fantastical story and getting lost in the rich and intricate worlds that authors create.

When it comes to my favorite subgenre within fantasy, there's no doubt that epic fantasy takes the top spot. I love the grand scale and epic storytelling of these novels, and the way they transport readers to fully-realized and immersive worlds. There's something special about following a cast of complex and diverse characters as they navigate a tumultuous and dangerous landscape, facing challenges and enemies at every turn.

One of the things I love most about epic fantasy is the way it allows authors to fully explore and world-build to their heart's content. These novels are usually long and sprawling, giving the author ample room to craft a fully realized and detailed world, complete with its own cultures, customs, and histories. When done well, this world-building can be truly breathtaking, and it's one of the aspects of epic fantasy that I find most captivating.

Of course, for me, no epic fantasy is complete without compelling characters and a well-crafted plot. I love following the journey of complex and fully-realized characters as they face challenges and overcome obstacles. Whether it's a group of misfits banding together to defeat a common enemy or a solitary hero on a quest to save the world, I'm always drawn in by the epic storylines and character arcs of these novels.

There are so many epic fantasy series that I have enjoyed over the years, but a few stand out as my all-time favorites. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan is a true masterpiece, with its intricate and multi-layered plot and fully-realized world. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson is another series that I have absolutely loved, with its epic storytelling and richly detailed world of Roshar. And of course, no list of epic fantasy series would be complete without mentioning The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien, a timeless classic that has had a profound influence on the genre.
